# Onboarding Flows

## Onboarding Philosophy

Onboarding has one job: get the user to log their first session. Everything else is secondary.

The user should feel, within 5 minutes of opening RiPeM for the first time:
1. "This was built for people like me"
2. "I know exactly what this does"
3. "I just logged my first session"

---

## Onboarding Screens

### Screen 1: Welcome
**Headline**: "Welcome to RiPeM"
**Body**: "The app built for builders. Document your project. Ask your AI. Share the journey."
**CTA**: "Let's go →"

---

### Screen 2: Add Your Vehicle
**Headline**: "What are you building?"
**Body**: "Start with your project vehicle. You can add more later."
**Input**: Year / Make / Model / Trim / Nickname (optional)
**CTA**: "Add Vehicle →"

---

### Screen 3: Log Your First Session
**Headline**: "Log your first session"
**Body**: "What did you work on last? Tap record and just talk — we'll handle the rest."
**CTA**: "Record Session 🎙️" or "Skip for now"

---

### Screen 4: Your Build Timeline
**Headline**: "Your build, documented"
**Body**: "Every session you log lives here. The more you log, the smarter your AI gets."
**Visual**: Show the session in the timeline (with extracted parts highlighted)
**CTA**: "See my build →"

---

### Screen 5 (Optional): AI Introduction
**Headline**: "Meet your AI mechanic"
**Body**: "Ask anything about your build. It knows your car, your history, and what thousands of other [Make Model] owners have done."
**CTA**: "Ask a question →" or "Later"

---

## Onboarding Copy Rules

1. Never use jargon that non-enthusiasts wouldn't know
2. Every screen has exactly one CTA — no decision fatigue
3. "Skip" is always available but never highlighted
4. Show value before asking for permissions (notifications come after first session)

## Post-Onboarding Push Notifications

### 24 hours after first session:
> "How'd the [part extracted] work out? Log your next session 🔧"

### 7 days with no session:
> "Your [Vehicle nickname] is waiting. What did you work on this week?"

### After 3 sessions:
> "You've got a solid build log going. Ready to show it off? Publish a build update."
