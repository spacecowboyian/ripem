# RiPeM User Personas

**Status**: v1.0 — April 2026
**Author**: UX Researcher (RiPeM)
**Purpose**: Ground the Product Manager's user stories and the engineering team's feature decisions in real-world user archetypes.

---

## How to Use This Document

Each persona represents a primary entry point into RiPeM. The three archetypes map directly to the product's three core entry points: **Documenter**, **Sharer**, and **Learner**. Real users may blend traits, but every feature decision should be anchored to at least one of these three.

---

## Persona 1: The Documenter — "Marcus"

> *"I just want to remember what I did to this car."*

### Demographics
- **Age**: 34
- **Occupation**: HVAC technician
- **Location**: Nashville, TN
- **Vehicle**: 2008 Subaru WRX (daily driver + weekend project)
- **Tech comfort**: Medium — uses apps daily but doesn't seek them out
- **Platform**: iOS primary

### Background
Marcus bought his WRX three years ago with 80,000 miles on it. He does most of his own maintenance — brakes, fluids, occasional bolt-ons. He's not building a track car; he's just trying to keep his car reliable and documented for resale. He's lost track of when he last changed his differential fluid, can't remember the brand of brake pads he used, and has a shoebox of receipts he never looks at.

### Goals
1. Know what he's done to his car and when
2. Remember part brands, part numbers, and costs
3. Have something to show a buyer if he ever sells
4. Get answers to basic maintenance questions fast

### Pain Points
- Spreadsheets are too slow to fill in after a grimy work session
- Photos are lost in his camera roll, not tied to a job
- He can't remember if he used synthetic or conventional oil last time
- Typing with greasy hands is nearly impossible

### Motivations
- Pride in keeping his car in good shape
- Practical value: accurate service history = higher resale price
- Doesn't need to share publicly — this is a private record

### Relationship with AI
- Skeptical at first, but converts when the AI gives an answer that clearly references his specific car and history
- Does not want a chatbot — wants quick, direct answers
- The "Dale Moment" equivalent for Marcus: AI says "Based on your last oil change 4,200 miles ago, you're about due — and you used Mobil 1 Full Synthetic 5W-30 last time."

### RiPeM Entry Point
**Documenter** — logs are the primary value, not social sharing.

### Subscription Tier Fit
Free tier. Casual maintenance tracker. Revenue: ad impressions (~$1.50/month).

### Primary Journey
Phase 2 (Voice Logging) is where Marcus lives. He taps record after a session, speaks for 30 seconds, and expects RiPeM to do the rest. The 12-screen onboarding is worth it only because Screen 12 makes him feel understood.

### Success Criteria for Marcus
- Logs at least 3 sessions in the first month
- Returns when he has a maintenance question ("When did I change my brakes?")
- Refers a friend ("I just use this app to track everything — you should too")

## Persona 2: The Sharer — "Jordan"

> *"I want people to see what I built."*

### Demographics
- **Age**: 26
- **Occupation**: Graphic designer (freelance)
- **Location**: Phoenix, AZ
- **Vehicle**: 1995 Honda Civic EG hatchback (dedicated build)
- **Tech comfort**: High — native to Instagram/TikTok, understands algorithms
- **Platform**: iOS and Instagram

### Background
Jordan has been building her Civic for two years. She's documented everything in Instagram stories and saved highlights, but the workflow is tedious: she has to write the caption herself, pick photos, remember what she did, and post manually. She has 4,200 followers on Instagram and knows that consistent posting is what grows an account, but the time cost is high. She wants a tool that does the heavy lifting so she can focus on the build.

### Goals
1. Publish build updates regularly without spending an hour writing captions
2. Grow her Instagram following with automotive-native content
3. Have a complete, beautiful record of her build
4. Get feedback and engagement from the community

### Pain Points
- Writing captions is time-consuming and she's not a "writer"
- Photos are scattered; it's hard to create a timeline narrative
- No automotive-native tool — she uses Notes app for tracking
- Watermarks feel like a punishment on other platforms (she resists it)

### Motivations
- Social recognition and audience growth
- The build itself is her identity; documenting it is sharing who she is
- Wants a tool that respects her aesthetic sensibility
- Open to watermarks if they look good and feel like a badge, not a billboard

### Relationship with AI
- Engages heavily with AI — asks about parts, modifications, what others are doing with EG Civics
- Appreciates that AI captions match her voice when she provides the logs
- Will test the AI to see if it knows car culture; dismisses it if it doesn't

### RiPeM Entry Point
**Sharer** — publishing is the primary value. Voice logging feeds content creation.

### Subscription Tier Fit
Chat Pro or Projects Add-on. Active builder, social creator. Revenue: premium subscriber ($5–7/month).

### Primary Journey
Phase 3 (Build Publishing + Discovery Feed) is Jordan's core loop. She logs sessions in Phase 2, but her motivation is the Phase 3 payoff — a clean auto-generated post ready for Instagram with one tap. The watermark must look like a brand partnership, not a spam tag.

### The Watermark Contract (Jordan's Lens)
Jordan will accept the watermark if it feels like:
- A badge of authenticity ("Built on RiPeM")
- Visually clean and small
- Something her followers might ask about ("What's RiPeM?")

She will resist it if it feels:
- Corporate or spammy
- Like it steals attention from her build

### Success Criteria for Jordan
- Publishes at least 1 post via RiPeM in the first two weeks
- At least one follower clicks through the watermark and downloads the app
- She upgrades to Chat Pro within 60 days

## Persona 3: The Learner — "Devon"

> *"I want to see what other people are doing with my exact car."*

### Demographics
- **Age**: 22
- **Occupation**: College student (mechanical engineering)
- **Location**: Columbus, OH
- **Vehicle**: 2004 Mazda RX-8 (just bought it, planning a build)
- **Tech comfort**: High — grew up digital, researches everything before buying
- **Platform**: iOS and web (uses web for deep research)

### Background
Devon just bought an RX-8 from Craigslist for $3,500. He knows the Renesis rotary engine has a reputation, and he's spent three weeks on forums trying to understand what he needs to do before the engine blows up. He's cross-referencing four different forums, two YouTube channels, and a Facebook group. He hasn't started any work yet — he's in research mode. He wants a single place that aggregates community knowledge specific to his car.

### Goals
1. Understand what other RX-8 owners have done (and what broke)
2. Get AI advice specific to his car and planned mods
3. Plan his build before he starts spending money
4. Eventually document and share his journey

### Pain Points
- Information is fragmented across forums, Reddit, Facebook, YouTube
- Forum posts are old; hard to tell if advice is still relevant
- Can't find a "RX-8 owner's guide to not blowing up your apex seals"
- No way to see what mods others have done for a specific budget

### Motivations
- Intellectual curiosity — wants to understand his car deeply before touching it
- Efficiency — doesn't want to make expensive mistakes
- Community connection — wants to find his people
- Long-term vision: wants to document his build once he starts

### Relationship with AI
- Highly engaged — will spend significant time in AI chat
- Tests the AI with hard questions ("What's the difference between the Series 1 and Series 2 injection maps?")
- Wants the AI to reference real community data, not generic advice
- Will churn immediately if AI gives a generic answer that ignores his specific car

### RiPeM Entry Point
**Learner** — discovery feed and AI assistant are the primary value hooks. Will convert to Documenter once he starts his build.

### Subscription Tier Fit
Free tier initially. High potential for Projects Add-on when he starts his build. Revenue: ad impressions + future upgrade.

### Primary Journey
Phase 1 (AI Garage Brain via Onboarding) and Phase 3 (Discovery Feed) are Devon's hooks. He needs to feel the AI is smarter than Google within the first 5 minutes. The discovery feed showing other RX-8 builds is the retention hook.

### Success Criteria for Devon
- Asks at least 5 AI questions in the first session
- Bookmarks or follows at least 3 other builds
- Returns within 7 days without a notification trigger
- Starts logging when he begins his build (converts from Learner to Documenter)

## Cross-Persona Notes

### Persona Overlap
Real users will blend these archetypes over time:
- Marcus (Documenter) may start sharing after building a history → becomes a Sharer
- Devon (Learner) will start logging once he begins work → becomes a Documenter
- Jordan (Sharer) uses AI heavily for research → also a Learner

### AI Calibration by Persona

| Persona | AI Tone | AI Depth | Response Speed |
|---------|---------|----------|---------------|
| Documenter (Marcus) | Direct, factual | Medium — answer + one tip | Fast — under 3s |
| Sharer (Jordan) | Engaged, car-culture fluent | High — voice matters | Medium — quality over speed |
| Learner (Devon) | Deep, technical, honest about uncertainty | Very high — cite community data | Medium — depth expected |

### Onboarding Completion by Persona

| Persona | Completion Likelihood | Drop-off Risk Screen | Mitigation |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|------------|
| Documenter | High (practical motivation) | Screen 3 (story) | Make story optional or short |
| Sharer | High (social motivation) | Screen 11 (privacy toggle) | Lead with public default; explain growth upside |
| Learner | Medium (no car yet or not ready) | Screen 7 (work completed) | Allow "just bought it / nothing yet" answer |
