# Assumptions

These are the core assumptions and hypotheses that underpin RiPeM's product, business, and strategy decisions. Each assumption should be validated through user research, data, or market evidence.

---

## Product Assumptions

### A-P1: Voice is the preferred logging method
**Assumption**: Car enthusiasts prefer speaking to typing when logging a build session, especially immediately after working on their car.
**Validation method**: Prototype test with 10–20 users in a garage context
**Status**: Unvalidated

### A-P2: AI extraction is accurate enough to be useful
**Assumption**: An LLM can reliably extract parts, costs, and time from a free-form voice transcript with >85% accuracy.
**Validation method**: Benchmark extraction against manually transcribed sessions
**Status**: Unvalidated

### A-P3: Users will publish if friction is low enough
**Assumption**: Enthusiasts want to share their builds but don't do so because current tools make it too hard.
**Validation method**: Observe publish rate during Phase 1 beta
**Status**: Unvalidated

---

## Business Assumptions

### A-B1: Car enthusiasts are willing to pay for a premium tier
**Assumption**: A meaningful segment (5–10%) of active users will subscribe to premium at a hobbyist-accessible price point.
**Validation method**: Introduce premium tier and measure conversion
**Status**: Unvalidated

### A-B2: The watermark drives measurable installs
**Assumption**: The RiPeM watermark on shared content will generate a viral coefficient (K-factor) of ≥ 0.3.
**Validation method**: Track attribution from deep links in watermark
**Status**: Unvalidated

### A-B3: Parts search ads generate meaningful revenue
**Assumption**: Contextual ads in parts search will generate sufficient revenue to offset AI API costs at scale.
**Validation method**: Model CPM/CPC rates from comparable automotive ad inventory
**Status**: Unvalidated

---

## Strategy Assumptions

### A-S1: Car enthusiasts on Facebook Groups are reachable and recruitable
**Assumption**: Power builders in niche Facebook Groups can be identified and recruited as beta users through direct outreach.
**Validation method**: Execute outreach and measure response rate
**Status**: Unvalidated

### A-S2: The cold-start problem can be solved through seeding
**Assumption**: Seeding 20–50 power builders with compelling builds will be sufficient to make the discovery feed valuable for new users at launch.
**Validation method**: Measure new user engagement with discovery feed at launch
**Status**: Unvalidated
