# Conversation Summary

## Origin of RipEm

RipEm was conceived as a solution to a real problem in the car enthusiast space: there is no dedicated, intelligent tool for documenting and sharing vehicle build projects.

## The Core Insight

Car enthusiasts spend thousands of dollars and hundreds of hours on project vehicles, but track everything in a scattered collection of spreadsheets, camera rolls, text threads, and Facebook posts. When they want advice, they turn to generic ChatGPT or post in Facebook Groups and wait for responses.

The insight: build a purpose-built app that combines voice-first logging, an automotive-context AI assistant, and a community sharing engine. Each of these alone has partial solutions. Together, they create something new.

## The Three Pillars

The original brainstorm identified three core user needs that became RipEm's three pillars:

1. **Document**: "I need to remember what I did and what I spent."
2. **Advise**: "I need specific advice about my car, not generic internet answers."
3. **Share**: "I want to show off my build and connect with other builders."

## The Naming Process

The name "RipEm" emerged from a brainstorm focused on:
- TikTok-native energy (short, two syllables)
- Car culture authenticity (RPM resonance)
- Social vernacular ("rip 'ems")

See [/naming/ripem_rationale.md](../naming/ripem_rationale.md) for full naming rationale.

## The Business Model Decision

The core business model was defined early:
- **Free tier must be genuinely useful** — not crippled to force upgrades
- **Ads only in contextually appropriate places** (parts search, never AI chat)
- **Watermark as growth, not punishment** — the viral loop depends on it

## The Growth Insight

The watermark loop was identified as the primary differentiator for organic growth:
> "Every published build is marketing for RipEm. The watermark turns every user into a growth engine."

This insight shaped the product: publishing had to be easy and the output had to be compelling enough to share.
