# Retention Strategy

## Why Retention Matters

Retention is the single most important metric. A car build is a long-term project (months to years). RiPeM's value compounds as a user logs more — more history means better AI, a richer timeline, and a more compelling build to publish.

## Retention Drivers

### 1. Progressive Value (Core)
- Day 1: User logs first session → sees their build timeline
- Week 1: User gets first AI insight based on their sessions
- Month 1: AI suggests publishing a build update
- Month 3+: Build history is valuable; switching cost is high

### 2. AI That Gets Better With Use
Every logged session makes the AI assistant more context-aware. Users who log more get better answers. This creates a **data flywheel** that rewards consistent use.

### 3. Social Accountability
- Followers on published builds create an audience to update
- Follower notifications encourage builders to keep logging
- Community comments and reactions on builds drive engagement

### 4. Milestone Notifications
- "You've logged 10 sessions — ready to publish your first build update?"
- "It's been 2 weeks since your last session — what have you been working on?"
- "Your build has 50 views — people are watching!"

## Churn Risk Factors

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| User finishes a project | Support multi-vehicle; suggest new project |
| AI answers feel generic | Improve context injection; add confidence scoring |
| No community content to discover | Seed content aggressively in Phase 1 |
| App feels unused between sessions | Push notifications for milestones and social activity |

## Retention Targets

| Cohort | Day 7 | Day 30 | Day 90 |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| Phase 1 | 30% | 20% | 15% |
| Phase 2+ | 40% | 25% | 20% |
