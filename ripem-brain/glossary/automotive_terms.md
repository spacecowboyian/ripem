# Automotive Terms

This glossary covers automotive and car culture terminology used in RiPeM documentation and AI prompts.

---

## A

**Aftermarket** — Parts or accessories not made by the original vehicle manufacturer (OEM). Aftermarket parts are a core part of the RiPeM use case.

**Alignment** — The adjustment of wheel angles to manufacturer or desired specifications. Often needed after suspension changes.

## B

**Boost** — Refers to positive pressure from a turbocharger or supercharger. "Boosted" = forced induction.

**Build** — Colloquial term for a modified or project vehicle and all the work done on it.

## C

**Coilovers** — Suspension components combining a coil spring and shock absorber in one unit. Common modification for handling improvement.

**Cold air intake (CAI)** — An aftermarket intake system that draws cooler, denser air into the engine for improved performance.

## D

**Daily driver** — A vehicle used for everyday transportation, sometimes also modified.

**Displacement** — The total volume of all engine cylinders, typically measured in liters or cubic centimeters.

**Downpipe** — The exhaust pipe directly after the turbocharger.

## E

**ECU / ECM** — Engine Control Unit / Engine Control Module. The computer that manages engine functions. Can be tuned (modified) for performance.

## F

**Fitment** — How well a part physically fits on a vehicle, or how wheels/tires fill the wheel arch.

**Forced induction** — Using a turbocharger or supercharger to force more air into the engine.

## H

**Headers** — Performance exhaust manifolds designed to improve exhaust flow and engine power.

## I

**Intake** — The air intake system for the engine. A cold air intake or short ram intake are common modifications.

## L

**Lift kit** — A suspension modification that raises the vehicle's ride height. Common on trucks and SUVs for off-road clearance.

## M

**Mod / Modification** — Any change made to a vehicle from its factory specification.

## O

**OEM** — Original Equipment Manufacturer. Refers to factory-original parts.

**Overland / Overlanding** — Style of vehicle adventure travel using a modified vehicle as a basecamp.

## P

**Project car** — A vehicle purchased specifically to be modified, restored, or built upon over time.

## R

**RPM** — Revolutions Per Minute. A measure of engine speed. Also the inspiration for the name "RiPeM."

**Resto-mod** — A vehicle that is restored to original appearance but modified with modern mechanical components.

**Roll cage** — A safety structure inside a vehicle protecting occupants in a rollover.

## S

**Short shifter** — A modified gear shifter that reduces the distance between gear changes.

**Stance** — A style of vehicle modification focused on lowered ride height and wheel fitment.

**Struts** — A structural suspension component (MacPherson strut is common on front-wheel-drive cars).

## T

**Track car** — A vehicle modified primarily for use on a race track.

**Tune / Tuning** — Modification of engine management parameters (ECU) to improve performance.

**Turbo / Turbocharger** — A device that forces additional air into the engine using exhaust gases, increasing power.

## W

**Wheel offset** — The distance between the wheel's mounting surface and the center of the wheel. Affects fitment.
