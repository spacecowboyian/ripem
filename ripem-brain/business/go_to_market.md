# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Phase 1: Launch & Early Adoption (Months 1–3)
**Goal**: 1,000–5,000 active users, 100+ published build logs

**Tactics**:
1. Soft launch with closed beta (target: 100 car enthusiasts)
2. Focus on early adopter feedback and product refinement
3. Identify 20–50 "power builder" accounts in niche Facebook Groups (e.g., Honda Civic mods, Subaru WRX builds, Miata owners)
4. Offer exclusive beta access and white-glove onboarding; offer free Premium for first 6 months
5. Capture their builds on RiPeM; use their content as social proof
6. Seed content on TikTok and Instagram using their real builds
7. Founder's landing page and referral program

**Target communities**:
- Facebook Groups: platform-specific mod communities (100K+ member groups)
- Reddit: r/projectcar, r/Justrolledintotheshop, platform-specific subs
- Discord car communities
- YouTube: Collaborate with small-to-mid car build channels
- Instagram: #projectcar, #cargram, platform hashtags

## Phase 2: Growth & Content (Months 4–9)
**Goal**: 10,000–50,000 users, 1,000+ active projects, viral content

**Tactics**:
1. Coordinate influencer builds going live simultaneously
2. Influencer partnerships with car YouTube channels — offer free Premium, ask them to document a build
3. App Store Optimization (ASO) for "car build app", "project car tracker"
4. Organic social media marketing (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts) — AI-generated short videos from community builds
5. Product Hunt launch
6. Content marketing: blog posts on best practices, car maintenance guides
7. Press outreach to automotive media (Motor Trend, Jalopnik, The Drive, Petrolicious)
8. Sponsorship of car events, shows, meetups

## Phase 3: Scale & Community (Months 10+)
**Goal**: 100,000+ users, sustainable growth, profitable

**Tactics**:
1. Paid user acquisition (Google Ads, Facebook/Instagram — only after product-market fit)
2. Car brand partnerships (manufacturers want enthusiast data insights)
3. Parts supplier integrations and affiliate revenue scaling
4. Community events (virtual and in-person meet-ups)
5. Podcast sponsorships (car enthusiast podcasts)
6. Strategic partnerships with forums (integrate data, cross-promote)

## Positioning & Messaging

**Core Message**: "Your car's story, organized and shared. Download the app. Start talking. Let AI do the work."

**Key Differentiators**:
- "No subscriptions required just to get started"
- "Voice-first: talk, don't type"
- "Your garage, your rules — keep it private or share"
- "AI-powered advice tailored to your car, not generic"

## Customer Acquisition Channels (Priority Order)

1. **Organic/Viral** (watermark-driven, Reddit, YouTube, TikTok, word-of-mouth) — ~$0 CAC
2. **Influencer partnerships** (YouTube car channels, build creators) — <$5/user
3. **Content marketing** (SEO-optimized guides, blog)
4. **Paid ads** (Google, Facebook, Instagram) — <$10/user; Phase 3 only
5. **Community sponsorships** (car meetups, forums)
6. **Media/PR** (tech blogs, car publications)

## Key Metrics to Track

- CAC by channel
- Viral coefficient (K-factor) from watermark shares
- Day 7 / Day 30 retention by acquisition channel
- Premium conversion rate
